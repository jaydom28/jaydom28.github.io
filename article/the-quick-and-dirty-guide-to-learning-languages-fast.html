The Quick and Dirty Guide to Learning Languages Fast

A book from A.G. Hawke about how green beret's learn a language as fast as possible. It goes over the order of things you should learn as well as what to learn in order to become "functional" in a language in 7 days and then significantly better in 30 days.

Day 1

  • Review all newly purchased materials for a few hours and then take a break
  • Sit down and outline book pages by category
  • Take another couple hours to fill in and begin each page with the appropriate headings
  • Consider the basic grammar points of your language as it pertains to just the basics of sentence construction

Day 2

  • Greetings, basics, and emergencies (hello, good-bye, my name is, bank, hotel, doctor, police)
  • Common expressions (How are you? How much is that?)

Day 3

  • Pronouns
  • Key Verbs (to be, to go, to eat, to buy)
  • Key Nouns (house, hotel, automobile, airport)

Day 4

  • Conjunctions (and, or, but)
  • Adjectives (good, hot, rainy)
  • Adverbs (slowly, quickly, under, over)

Day 5

  • Synonyms (furious/mad, happy/glad, helpful/useful)
  • Antonyms (hot/cold, slow/fast, good/bad)
  • Numbers, time, weather
  • Commands and imperatives

Days 6 and 7

  • Cram as much grammar as possible in on day 6 so that you can simply review on day 7
    • Past-tense rules
    • present-tense results
    • future-tense rules
    • case
    • gender
    • plural and singular
    • articles
    • possession
